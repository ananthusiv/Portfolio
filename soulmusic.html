Introduction

"I feel overwhelmed, and music is my escape, but finding the right song is exhausting sometimes." – 27-year-old user

The product
Soul Music is a wellness-focused music app that enhances users' emotional well-being by curating personalized playlists based on their current mood. The AI-driven system uses mood assessments and emotional inputs to create tailored listening experiences, helping users manage emotions through music.

The app focuses on integrating music therapy principles with AI-driven personalization, catering to users seeking relaxation, motivation, or comfort in their daily lives.

The users
Target audience: Adults aged 18-35 who rely on music as a form of emotional self-care. These users often experience high levels of stress and use music to manage their mental and emotional health.

The business goal
We aimed to improve user engagement by encouraging users to frequently use the app to check their mood and discover new playlists aligned with their emotional state.

How might we help users find music that enhances their emotional well-being?

Using AI for better personalization
User research showed that most users listen to music based on their emotional needs, but often struggle to find songs that match their exact mood. Our challenge was to integrate a mood assessment feature that could easily identify their current emotional state and suggest music that resonates with how they feel.

The proposed solution
AI-driven mood assessment feature. We introduced an interactive mood assessment tool and a prompt input for users to select or describe their emotions before generating playlists tailored to their emotional needs. By combining this with AI and user data, we aimed to create a seamless experience for users seeking emotional relief through music.

An unexpected issue

"I saw the 'Create with Soul AI' button, but I didn’t realize it was for personalized playlists."

What didn’t work?

Users noticed the "Create with Soul AI" button, but didn’t immediately connect it with creating mood-based playlists. The text near the button wasn’t enough to convey the full function to some users. Users assumed the feature was more about standard playlist generation, not tailored to their emotions.

What did work?

By adding more context around the AI's role in curating personalized playlists, we increased user engagement. Additionally, simplifying the button design and labeling led to more intuitive usage.

Helping users discover mood-based playlists

"It would be great if there was a better way to find playlists for my emotions."

Priority Revisions

Since users didn’t have trouble finding the button, the challenge was clarifying the purpose of the "Create with Soul AI" feature, ensuring they understood its connection to personalized, mood-based playlists.

Idea 1: Add a brief explanation or tooltip

  • Pro: A small tooltip or short description appearing when users hover or tap the button could explain that the AI creates playlists based on their mood.
  • Con: Too much text may clutter the UI or overwhelm users.

Idea 2: Integrate mood check-in with the button

  • Pro: Introducing a quick mood check-in step before playlist creation would help users connect their emotional state with the playlist. This can provide clarity on how the AI works with their input.
  • Con: Adding an extra step might feel like friction for users who want a fast experience.

Idea 3: Use visual elements to enhance understanding

  • Pro: Adding an emoticon or small icons that represent different moods alongside the button could make it clearer that the AI-generated playlists are mood-based.
  • Con: This could affect the simplicity of the button design and may require careful balancing of visuals.

Where do we go from here?

We plan to continue improving the AI system to make music suggestions even more accurate and finely tuned to users' unique emotional states. Additionally, we’re considering a broader set of user feedback loops to further refine and enhance playlists based on emotional shifts throughout the day.

Next steps: I recommend testing the updated UI with new participants to see if these changes make the "Create with Soul AI" feature clearer.

Metrics to track:

  • How often users click the "Create with Soul AI" button after the revisions?
  • Do users engage more with the personalized playlists once they understand the feature better?
  • How has the change impacted user satisfaction and retention?

What I learned:

The importance of clear communication in UX. While the button was visible, making sure users fully understood the functionality required thoughtful adjustments to text and visual elements.
